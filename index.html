Afghan Entrepreneur: Balancing Studies, Family and Business

His iPhone shows him here’s a way, but he sees no way ahead. He looks around. It’s a ghost street. He can only see a police officer.

Inayat Rahman, 29, a grad student of international development at Brandeis University, has been in the U.S. for only two weeks. He needs to ask the officer for directions, despite the innate fear of police he has brought with him from his home country, Afghanistan. The officer grabs Rahman’s iPhone, looks at its screen and says, “I have CSS disease.
